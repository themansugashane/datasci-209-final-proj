Exploring Life Expectancy: Going Beyond GDP

Our Project

What truly determines how long people live? While GDP is often treated as a benchmark for national well-being, it tells only part of the story. Our project, Exploring Life Expectancy: Going Beyond GDP, aims to dig deeper.

We explore why countries with similar levels of economic output can have very different health outcomes. By focusing on three key drivers of education spending, healthcare investment and access, and CO₂ emissions, we examine how social policy and environmental conditions shape life expectancy across nations.

We aim to uncover meaningful insights that explain how targeted investments in people and the environment contribute to longer, healthier lives. Our goal is to support better decision-making. By identifying the factors that most strongly influence longevity, we hope to provide policymakers, researchers, and public health advocates with data they can use to design more effective and equitable strategies.

Areas of Focus

Educational Spend

Annual public spending on education, measured as a percentage of GDP or per capita, across countries and over time.

Healthcare Spending and Access

National healthcare expenditures and universal health coverage scores by country and year.

CO₂ Emissions

Per capita carbon dioxide emissions from fossil fuels and industry, reported annually at the country level.

Created by Jagriti Singh, Mina Baghai, Shane Brooks, and Taylor DeSantis
