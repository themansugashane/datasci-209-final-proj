Exploring Life Expectancy: Going Beyond GDP

Our Project

What truly determines how long people live? While GDP is often treated as a benchmark for national well-being, it tells only part of the story. Our project, Exploring Life Expectancy: Going Beyond GDP, aims to dig deeper. We explore why countries with similar levels of economic output can have very different health outcomes. By focusing on three key drivers of education spending, healthcare investment and access, and CO₂ emissions, we examine how social policy and environmental conditions shape life expectancy across nations.

We aim to uncover meaningful insights that explain how targeted investments in people and the environment contribute to longer, healthier lives. Our goal is to give policymakers, researchers, and public health advocates an exploratory foundation they can build on. One that helps them investigate why certain strategies succeed, where others fall short, and how their own country's policies and outcomes have evolved over time.

By drawing on our four visualizations of regional comparisons, global peer comparisons, trend comparisons over time, and contrasts among countries with similar GDP, we provide a multi-layered perspective that goes well beyond simple economic metrics. These visuals allow us to highlight patterns such as how neighboring countries with shared histories diverge in health outcomes, which nations with similar spending or emissions can serve as policy models, and how shifts in investment or environmental impact correspond with changes in life expectancy over the past two decades. Together, these visuals help reveal which strategies consistently lead to better health outcomes and which gaps may be holding countries back. Ultimately, we hope to support exploratory decision-making by equipping users with clear, data-driven evidence that can guide more effective and equitable decisions and research.

Data Source

The data for this project comes from Our World in Data, which provides a wide range of country-level datasets used to build each visual on this webpage. We used their publicly available indicators on yearly life expectancy, healthcare spending, universal healthcare coverage, education spending, CO₂ emissions, and GDP. These datasets span the past two decades and were combined using country and year to create a unified view to analyze how these factors relate to life expectancy among different countries over time.

Areas of Focus

Educational Spend

Annual public spending on education, measured as a percentage of GDP or per capita, across countries and over time.

Healthcare Spending and Access

National healthcare expenditures and universal health coverage scores by country and year.

CO₂ Emissions

Per capita carbon dioxide emissions from fossil fuels and industry, reported annually at the country level.
